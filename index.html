About Myself:

As indicated in my resume, I once pursued a career in aviation. I received my commercial pilot’s license from Algonquin Flight Center along with other ratings and licenses. I have also gained valuable work experience from my employment with Transwest Air, a small airlines that operates in Saskatchewan. This experience includes micro-management, dispatching, billing, computer and multiple ground handling skills. After being laid off, due to lack of work within this company, I decided to enroll at Carleton University for computer science. Although I still have an interest in aviation, my future at this time is focused in the field of computer science

In January 2015, I graduated from Carleton University with a Bachelors degree in computer science. My chosen stream at Carleton was called mobile and social networking applications. During my time at Carleton I had three co-op placements. My first coop placement was for 8-months at Public Works Government Services. Here I gained experience in web and mobile phone development. My second coop placement was at The Ottawa Hospital for 4-months. Here I continued to develop my SQL and web development skills. My last coop position was at HCN (Hotel Communication Network Inc.) as an Android developer. I have learned many computer languages at school and at my co-op placements. Some of them include Java, C, Objective-C, JS, Assembly, and HTML. After graduating, I continued to work fulltime with my last co-op placement at HCN.
